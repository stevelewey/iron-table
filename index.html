The Problem Worth Naming

You Are More Isolated
Than You Realize

You have employees who depend on you, clients who pay you, and friends who care about you. What you likely do not have is a table of peers who understand your world, share your challenges, and will tell you the truth.

That isolation is not a character flaw. It is the structural reality of leadership. When you sit at the top of your organization — or carry your business alone — the normal feedback loops disappear. The hard questions go unasked. The blind spots accumulate quietly.

"Most marketplace leaders I have worked with have integrity, focus, and work ethic. What they lack is a functioning environment — and that includes the right people around them."

— STEVE LEWEY

The locust has no king — and yet it advances in ranks. It accomplishes together what no individual locust could alone. That is not an accident of nature. It is a principle. And it applies directly to the way marketplace leaders are designed to work.

Fit Matters

This Table Is
Not For Everyone

An application is required — not to create barriers, but because fit protects everyone at the table. The quality of the conversation depends on who sits down.

This Is For You If

  • You own a business or carry significant leadership responsibility
  • You are tired of making hard decisions without a trusted sounding board
  • You are ready to be challenged — not just encouraged
  • You value confidentiality and will honor it for others
  • Faith and marketplace life are not separate categories for you
  • You will show up prepared and engaged, not just present

This Is Not For You If

  • You are primarily looking for a networking or referral group
  • You want validation more than honest feedback
  • Consistency is difficult — life makes regular attendance unlikely
  • You are not yet operating with real leadership responsibility
  • You prefer a lecture or curriculum format over peer dialogue
